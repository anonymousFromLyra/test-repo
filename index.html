Lyra: Hierarchical Alignment for
Autoregressive Audio Generation

via Variational Policy Optimization

Autoregressive models over hierarchical Residual Vector Quantization (RVQ) tokens have emerged as the dominant approach for high-fidelity audio generation. We propose Lyra, a reinforcement learning post-training framework that aligns RVQ token prediction with the waveform-level acoustic distribution by optimizing a hierarchical ELBO over RVQ layers. Lyra employs subset-wise teacher forcing to realize a hierarchically factorized variational posterior and layer-decomposable acoustic rewards under GRPO for stable cross-layer credit assignment. Lyra reduces FAD by 19.3% on average and lifts SongEval from 3.64 to 4.00, at only 24% of the cost of conventional free-running GRPO.

§ 01

HeartMuLa-3B

HeartMuLa uses a Global-Local Transformer architecture over HeartCodec (8 RVQ codebooks, 48 kHz). Lyra is applied to the residual subspace cb1–cb7, reducing FAD from 1.130 to 0.859 (−23.98%) and achieving a 59.7% human preference rate over the base model. Samples span English and Mandarin Chinese prompts from the Muse dataset.

§ 02

YuE (7B + 1B)

YuE adopts a two-stage pipeline with XCodec tokenization: Stage 1 predicts the primary codebook; Stage 2 predicts the remainder as a flat token sequence. Lyra is applied independently to each stage, reducing FAD from 4.631 to 3.327 (−28.2%) and lifting SongEval from 2.84 to 3.47, with a 63.6% human preference rate over the base model.
